4. Data Types in Funky Trees

docs / tutorial / data /

Written by SnoWFLakE0s

At a fundamental level, programming deals with the manipulation of data. In doing this, we come to realize that there is more than one type of data. In programming, this is commonly referred to as simply “datatypes”. In this section of the tutorial, we will cover the three types of data available for use in the Funky Trees language, how they interact, and the precautions that must be taken to obtain the desired result.

Before explaining each datatype, it is important that you understand why datatypes are important. Similar to how you can't mix water with oil, datatypes cannot be mixed without special procedures. By first understanding what datatypes you are working with and what datatypes an operation will result in, you can efficiently manage your programming and prevent causing errors that will cost you time and energy trying to solve.

A. Numbers

The first datatype we will cover is the most commonly used datatype in Funky Trees. Numbers, or to be more technical, floats, are one of the three datatypes in Funky Trees. They are what they sound like, a numeric value. However, one distinction you should note is that all numbers in Funky Trees have a decimal portion attached to them (at least implicitly). If you do a calculation in Funky Trees like 1+1, the internal processing will treat them as 1.0000... + 1.0000.... This distinction is important to make, as certain popular programming practices rely on data that is strictly integer-based (that is to say, no decimals attached).

However, for the average beginning Funky Trees programmer there is no need to fret about such details.
